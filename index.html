Fridgenator

UI/UX Design

Fridgenator is a smart way to track your food inventory and find best recipes for in-home cooking.

My role

Design an app with UI/UX components. Conduct user research and user testing, Solve problems for the users main paint points. Design wireframes and create prototype. Our group collaborated all the way up to the final prototype, which I went in to finish out.

“How do we help adults save time and money by helping keep their food inventory organized?”

Challenge

40% of all food in the United States is wasted. As hardworking adults it is hard to find time to plan and cook a full meal each week without wasting part of your in house food items. Here are some stats on food waste in America:

  • $1,700 / annually per household the amount of money we are literally throwing in the trash
  • 40 million tons / annually food waste in the United States
  • 55% of the shoppers are eating at home more often since the pandemic began

Solution

We believe tracking in-home food inventory and having access to the recipes based off on in-house food items will allow working adults, who enjoy cooking but not planning, to avoid wastage of food items, manage their grocery shopping more efficiently, and boost conscious eating.

User Reasearch

We conducted five user interviews to discover all important features we should include and the pain points each user has when it comes to cooking meals at home. Here were the key takeaways found from conducting these interviews:

Interviews

“Sometimes I wonder why so many of the recipes don’t actually work? A new recipe goes a lot more smoothly if you can learn the tricks and quirks of the techniques from someone who really knows what to do. “

“I am not utilizing everything that I have in my fridge or pantry. And as a result I have lots of food waste every week.”

“We try to cook weekday but the choice of meal depends on our daily routine and how tired we are. So we can cook something easy or, if we have more time, we can do something fun. “

Findings

  • Tasty images and videos are an essential part of the app.
  • Users have a repertoire of favorite meals but they also enjoy experimenting.
  • Users like the opportunity to create a shopping list with one click.
  • Users find it beneficial to keep tracking all in-home food inventory to save budget and time.
  • Users want to have suggested recipes based on ingredients they have at home.

User Testing/ Iterations

We conducted user tests to determine whether the user flow made sense and where they might run into problems. We created multiple tasks for each user to complete to observe whether they were navigating through the application at ease. We tested with our mid-fidelity prototype and with the feedback we recieved, I implemented that into my final high-fidelity design.

Tasks

The user had already created an account and is ready to log.

  1. Start with main button to begin logging
  2. Pick which area you would like to begin logging food items
  3. Swipe through each place of logging
  4. Submit

The user has already logged in house food items and is ready to start finding recipes.

  1. Start with choosing the recipes button
  2. Answer the 3 basic preferences for recipes
  3. Submit preferences
  4. Answer option screen
  5. Look through recipes and click a single one
  6. Browse the recipe screen

Changes Made

1. Took out the feature of food items being stored in fridge, freezer, or pantry.

We found users felt it was unecessary and too confusing to have the food items go to specific destinations. Allowing them to just add the food item directly to one main storage unit still functions efficiently, but without the confusion for the user.

Before

After

2. Moved the filters to a pop up menu instead of including on main page.

Not every user wants to filter their recipe results, so having that be the first option users see leads to discouragement of continuing with the application. I included the filter options as a pop up instead to keep the options still available.

Before

After

3. Changed the function of adding food items to grocery list.

I took out the option of showing the user which food item they already have in storage because it did not allow for the user to add the same food item to their grocery list. With this new addition, the user is able to add any food item to their grocery list without confusion.

Conclusion

We came up with future additions to make our application come more to life. The user will be able to add certain ingredients, from the recipes page, to their grocery list. They then will be able to go into the grocery list feature and organize it out from there. Users will be able to switch the mode of the recipe feature and be able to search a certain recipe and obtain a whole grocery list for that meal. They can add this list under the grocery list feature.

Overall our project was very exciting to work on. We came up with many solutions to help with the organization of users home-food life. With the time frame given, we got all the way up to a working prototype to showcase one feature, but we still have many improvements to add.
